# BAC3-BIOINFORMATICS-APHA
<p>  The Bioinformatics Group within Bacteriology at the  <a href="https://www.gov.uk/government/organisations/animal-and-plant-health-agency"     target="_blank"     rel="noopener noreferrer">     Animal and Plant Health Agency (APHA)  </a>  develops computational tools, scalable analysis pipelines, and reproducible workflows for  bacterial genomics and antimicrobial resistance (AMR) surveillance.</p><p>  Our work supports genomic epidemiology, public health investigations, veterinary and food  microbiology, and applied bacteriology through the integration of bioinformatics, data science,  and microbial genomics.</p><p>  We work across a wide range of applications including:</p><ul>  <li>Whole genome sequencing (WGS)</li>  <li>Metagenomics</li>  <li>Phylogenetics and evolutionary analysis</li>  <li>Outbreak investigation</li>  <li>Comparative genomics</li>  <li>AMR detection and characterisation</li>  <li>Workflow and pipeline development</li>  <li>Data visualisation and analytics</li>  <li>Reproducible and scalable bioinformatics</li></ul><p>  This organisation hosts pipelines, software tools, analysis frameworks, visualisation utilities,  and supporting resources developed by the team to enable reproducible, transparent, and  collaborative scientific analysis.</p><p>  Our repositories include workflows built with technologies such as Python, Nextflow, R,  and containerised environments designed for scalable and portable bioinformatics.</p><p>  We are committed to promoting open, reproducible, and collaborative bioinformatics  for scientific research and surveillance.</p>
